## The Problem with English-First Speech Technology

Most automatic speech recognition systems are trained predominantly on English data. The result? Technology that works brilliantly for a fraction of the world's population — and leaves everyone else behind. India alone has over 22 scheduled languages, hundreds of dialects, and more than a billion people who communicate primarily in languages other than English.

## Building for Low-Resource Languages

When you're working with a language that has limited digital text corpora and even less audio data, every design decision matters. Transfer learning from multilingual models like Whisper or MMS gets you a starting point, but the last mile — the dialect variations, the code-switching, the domain-specific vocabulary — requires careful, community-driven data collection and fine-tuning.

## Reading Fluency as a Lens

One of our core use cases at Wadhwani AI is building reading fluency assessment tools that help measure learning outcomes for children at scale across India. When a child reads aloud, the system needs to accurately transcribe what was said — in their regional language, with their accent, at their reading level — and provide meaningful feedback to teachers and administrators. Getting ASR right here isn't just a technical challenge; it has direct implications for educational equity.

## What Comes Next

The path forward involves community-driven data collection, open-source tooling, and deep collaboration with educators on the ground. Better speech technology for Indic languages isn't just a research problem — it's a prerequisite for building AI systems that genuinely serve India's population.
